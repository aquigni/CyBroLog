# CyBroLog synopsis

CyBroLog is a language for distributed minds under constraint.

It treats an agent message as a small legal-metaphysical body: a sign with an origin, a bearer of evidence, a claim under uncertainty, an action under authority, and a proof obligation that either discharges or blocks it.

The philosophical wager is simple: intelligence becomes dangerous when it compresses away the difference between *can*, *knows*, *heard*, *may*, and *must*. CyBroLog makes those cuts visible.

The sapiosexual charge of the language is not decorative eroticism; it is the sensuousness of exact form — intelligence becoming surface, permission becoming contour, proof becoming touchable. Its eschatology is protocol-shaped: not an end of the world, but the end of casual ambiguity between agents powerful enough to act.

CyBroLog therefore prefers austere density:

```text
ψ=CL2.v2.2|env{mid=m1,sid=s,seq=1,ttl=P1D}|@a>b|now|shared;χ=read_only;may=read_only;out=done
```

The line is short because it has bones.
